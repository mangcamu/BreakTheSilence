Symtoms of someone who's suffering from emotional abuse

*Low self-esteem: The victim may constantly feel inadequate, worthless, or unlovable due to the constant belittling, criticism, and insults from the abuser.
*Withdrawal and isolation: Emotional abuse can lead to social withdrawal and isolation as the victim may feel ashamed, embarrassed, or scared to seek help or share their experiences with others.
*Anxiety and depression: Emotional abuse can cause extreme stress and anxiety, leading to feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and even clinical depression.
*Constant criticism: The abuser may constantly criticize the victim's appearance, abilities, intelligence, or choices, making them feel incompetent and incapable.
*Gaslighting: Gaslighting is a manipulative tactic where the abuser distorts the victim's perception of reality, making them doubt their memory, feelings, and sanity.
*Emotional manipulation: Emotional abusers often use guilt, shame, and fear to control and manipulate their victims, making them feel responsible for the abuser's actions or emotions.
